1) “Pre-Implementation Steps” Readiness Micro-Module

Rise 360 Implementation Onboarding Micro Learning

Problem: Inconsistent program delivery among decentralized implementers was leading to "program drift," which compromised the integrity of the data and diminished the overall impact of the substance prevention interventions.

Solution: Developed a standardized, asynchronous training suite that utilizes "locked-sequence" learning paths. This ensures every implementer masters core protocols and verifies their competency before being cleared for delivery.

The Impact: Standardized the onboarding process for new implementers to ensure 100% alignment with program protocols before they reach the field.

Launch module

2) Branching Scenario: Health Screening → Personalized Feedback

Storyline 360 Branching Personalized Feedback

Problem: Standard "one-size-fits-all" wellness videos often lead to low engagement because the content isn't relevant to the individual’s specific health habits or risks.

Solution: Developed a branching health-screening interaction that delivers tailored guidance based on real-time learner responses. This creates a "Brief Intervention" model within the eLearning environment.

The Impact: Increased learner relevance by filtering out unnecessary content and providing a personalized action plan, improving the likelihood of post-training behavior change.

Launch scenario

3) Economics Data Literacy Module: Inflation & Interest Rates

Rise 360 Data literacy Higher Ed

Problem: Learners often struggle to connect abstract economic theories with real-world data, leading to a gap in practical financial decision-making skills.

Solution: Built an interactive module that guides users through live FRED data charts to analyze the relationship between inflation and interest rates, culminating in a data-driven policy recommendation.

The Impact: Successfully translated high-level financial concepts into an intuitive digital experience, demonstrating the ability to design training for technical subject matter experts.

Launch lesson

4) Workbook Design Sample: Healthy Lifestyles (High School)

Canva Student Workbook Activity Design

Problem: Digital-only learning can lack the "tactile" engagement needed for deep reflection and long-term retention of information.

Solution: Designed a clean, print-ready participant workbook that aids in reinforcing program goals. The layout uses structured activities to guide learners through the behavioral change process.

The Impact: Provided a cohesive multi-modal learning experience that supports both independent study and classroom facilitation.
